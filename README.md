<h1>NBI Online Appointment: Step-by-Step Guide (2026)</h1>

<h2>Introduction</h2>
<p>Booking an <a href="https://nbiclear.ph/" target="_blank">NBI Online Appointment</a> is one of the most searched topics in the Philippines — and for good reason. Many first-time applicants feel confused about where to start, which branch to choose, or how to pay. If you have been searching for a clear and simple guide, you are in the right place.</p>
<p>In this article, we will walk you through every step of the NBI Online Appointment process — from registration to your actual branch visit. By the end of this guide, you will know exactly what to do and what to bring so there are no surprises on your appointment day.</p>

<h2>What You Need Before You Start</h2>
<p>Before opening the NBI website, make sure you have the following items ready:</p>
<ul>
  <li>A valid government-issued ID (Passport, Driver's License, UMID, PhilSys ID, or similar)</li>
  <li>An active email address — all confirmations will be sent here</li>
  <li>A working mobile number for verification</li>
  <li>Payment method ready — GCash, Maya, online banking, or over-the-counter</li>
  <li>A stable internet connection</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Important:</strong> Make sure your full name, date of birth, and other personal details are exactly the same as what appears on your ID. Even a small spelling difference can cause a "hit" status, which may delay your clearance release.</p>

<h2>Step-by-Step: How to Book Your NBI Online Appointment</h2>

<h3>Step 1 — Go to the Official NBI Website</h3>
<p>Open your browser and go to the official NBI Clearance website at <strong>clearance.nbi.gov.ph</strong>. Do not use any third-party or unofficial websites, as these may charge extra fees or steal your personal information.</p>

<h3>Step 2 — Create Your Account or Log In</h3>
<p>If you are a first-time applicant, click on "Register" and fill in your personal details carefully. These include your full name, date of birth, place of birth, civil status, and contact information. If you already have an existing account, simply log in using your registered email and password.</p>

<h3>Step 3 — Fill In Your Personal Information</h3>
<p>After logging in, the system will ask you to complete your applicant profile. Double-check every detail before moving to the next step. This is the most important part — errors here can cause delays or a hit status.</p>

<h3>Step 4 — Select Your NBI Branch and Set an Appointment</h3>
<p>Choose your preferred NBI branch from the list. It is recommended to pick a branch closest to your location. After selecting a branch, choose an available date and time slot. Try to book at least 3 to 5 days in advance, as slots fill up quickly especially in Metro Manila.</p>

<h3>Step 5 — Pay the Required Fee</h3>
<p>The current fee for NBI Clearance is <strong>Php 130.00</strong>, plus a small transaction fee depending on your chosen payment method. Available payment channels include:</p>
<ul>
  <li>GCash</li>
  <li>Maya (PayMaya)</li>
  <li>Online banking (BPI, BDO, Metrobank, etc.)</li>
  <li>Over-the-counter (7-Eleven, Bayad Center, etc.)</li>
</ul>
<p>After successful payment, you will receive a reference number. Save this number — you will need it on your appointment day.</p>

<h3>Step 6 — Save or Print Your Appointment Confirmation</h3>
<p>The system will send a confirmation email with your appointment details. Save a screenshot or print the confirmation page. It will contain your reference number, selected branch, date, and time slot.</p>

<h3>Step 7 — Visit Your Selected NBI Branch on Appointment Day</h3>
<p>On the day of your appointment, arrive at the branch at least 15 to 20 minutes early. Bring the following:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Printed or digital copy of your appointment confirmation</li>
  <li>Your reference number</li>
  <li>At least one valid government-issued ID (original, not photocopy)</li>
  <li>Payment receipt or confirmation screenshot</li>
</ul>

<h3>Step 8 — Biometrics and Photo Capture</h3>
<p>Once inside the branch, you will go through biometric scanning — this includes fingerprinting and photo capture. The process is quick and usually takes only a few minutes.</p>

<h3>Step 9 — Receive Your NBI Clearance</h3>
<p>If your record comes back clean with no hits, your NBI Clearance will be released on the same day. If there is a hit — which means your name is similar to someone with a court record — you may need to return after a few working days for further verification.</p>

<h2>Common Mistakes to Avoid</h2>
<p>Many applicants experience delays because of easily avoidable errors. Here are the most common ones:</p>
<ol>
  <li>Entering incorrect personal information during registration</li>
  <li>Missing the appointment without rescheduling ahead of time</li>
  <li>Forgetting to bring a valid ID on appointment day</li>
  <li>Not saving or printing the appointment confirmation</li>
  <li>Using an unofficial website to book an appointment</li>
</ol>

<h2>Tips to Speed Up Your NBI Appointment Process</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Book your appointment early in the morning — slots open daily and fill up fast</li>
  <li>Choose a weekday slot if possible — branches are less crowded than weekends</li>
  <li>Arrive early at the branch — being late may result in losing your slot</li>
  <li>Keep your reference number saved on your phone and also written down</li>
  <li>Bring two valid IDs just in case one is questioned</li>
</ul>
